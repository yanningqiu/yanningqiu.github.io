Water quality science with an AI-native research workflow.

I am Yanning Qiu, an Assistant Professor in Water Quality and Data Science working at the Aquatic Ecology and Water Quality Management Group, Wageningen University & Research.

My current research focuses on the risk assessment of microplastics on human health and the environment.

I am particularly interested in integrating artificial intelligence into scientific research, including the use of large language models (LLMs) and computer vision (CV) to advance literature synthesis, structured data extraction, and environmental monitoring.

Research Themes

Three threads that shape my work.

I work where environmental questions meet computable evidence, reproducible data pipelines, and field-facing monitoring systems.

Risk and evidence synthesis

Building better ways to assemble, harmonize, and interpret evidence on microplastic exposure and effects across environmental and human health contexts.

AI for scientific workflows

Using LLMs to support literature review, structured data extraction, and quality control in research workflows that need to scale without losing rigor.

Vision-based monitoring

Exploring how computer vision can turn images from the field into usable environmental signals for monitoring, assessment, and decision support.

Current Direction

From scientific literature to operational environmental data.

A recurring theme in my work is connecting messy real-world evidence with structured, reusable analytical systems. That includes reviewing scientific literature at scale, extracting data in a machine-readable way, and connecting those outputs to quantitative risk assessment.

I am especially interested in workflows where ecological expertise and AI tooling reinforce each other, rather than being treated as separate parts of the research process.

Outside Research

When I step away from work, I am usually hiking, skiing, birdwatching, or photographing wildlife. That same curiosity about observation and patterns continues to shape how I think about science.
